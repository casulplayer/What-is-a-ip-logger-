Understanding IP Logging & Online Safety

Learn how malicious websites, scam links, and attackers may try to collect IP addresses — and the best ways to protect yourself online.

What Is an IP Address?

Your public IP address is what websites and services can see when you connect online. Think of it like an internet address that helps data reach your device.

How Attackers Try To Get IPs

Some malicious people trick users into opening links that automatically reveal a visitor's public IP.

  • Fake giveaways
  • Scam websites
  • Shortened or hidden URLs
  • "Free download" pages
  • Social engineering tricks

Malicious Sites

Some sites are designed to mislead people into clicking links or downloading unsafe software.

  • Fake login pages
  • Sketchy mod or cheat downloads
  • Sites pretending to be trusted
  • Scare tactics or fake warnings

What Can Someone Do With an IP?

Usually, an IP alone does not reveal your exact address. However, it may show:

  • Approximate location
  • Your internet provider
  • General region or city

Sometimes people misuse this information for harassment or intimidation online.

Warning Signs

  • Someone begging you to click a link
  • Random shortened URLs
  • "Download this now" messages
  • Threats about “pulling your IP”
  • Sites that look suspicious

Ways To Stay Safer

  • Avoid random links
  • Verify websites before clicking
  • Keep software updated
  • Use strong passwords
  • Enable 2FA when possible

How VPNs Help Protect You

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) routes your internet traffic through another secure server.

Instead of websites seeing your normal home IP, they usually see the VPN server's IP instead.

  • Helps reduce home IP exposure
  • Adds privacy on public Wi-Fi
  • Makes tracking harder for some sites
  • Can reduce harassment risks

Suggested options:

  • Proton VPN — privacy-focused with a free plan
  • Cloudflare WARP — improves privacy & connection security

A VPN helps with privacy, but safe browsing habits still matter.

Important Reminder

Websites naturally receive a visitor’s public IP when visited. Be careful of strangers pressuring you into clicking random links, especially on Discord, games, or social media.
