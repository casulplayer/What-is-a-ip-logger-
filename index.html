Understanding IP Logging & Online Safety

Learn how malicious websites and attackers may attempt to collect IP addresses — and how to stay safer online.

What Is an IP Address?

Your public IP is the address websites see when you connect. It helps internet traffic reach your device and is commonly visible to websites you visit.

How Attackers Try To Get IPs

Malicious actors sometimes try to trick people into opening links or websites that reveal their public IP address automatically.

  • Fake giveaway links
  • "Free download" pages
  • Shortened or hidden URLs
  • Social engineering & scams

What Can They Do?

An IP address alone usually does not reveal your exact home address, but it can sometimes show an approximate location, internet provider, or be misused for harassment attempts.

Warning Signs

  • Someone demanding you click a random link
  • Suspicious shortened URLs
  • "Download this to win" scams
  • People threatening to "pull your IP"

Ways To Stay Safer

  • Avoid suspicious links
  • Verify websites before clicking
  • Keep devices updated
  • Use privacy tools if needed
  • Block/report harassment

Important

This page is for cybersecurity awareness and education. Websites naturally receive a visitor's public IP when visited. Be cautious of strangers pressuring you into clicking links.
